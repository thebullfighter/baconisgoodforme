The Problem

My dataset doesn’t identify an immediate problem however from empirical research, the buildings that are around the areas of population by country, lack culture representation. All buildings from Penrith to Sydney are squared and have that 30s social housing feel. My main focus is Turkey Males and Females. According to the dataset they are all mainly situated in Auburn. My solution will be a blueprint for all future buildings that hold culture significance.



Playful

Fond of games and amusement; Lighthearted. I'm using playful in my design as it's lacking in the current landscape. There's too much structure.

Inspirational

The result of too much structure means that there is no room to be inspired. Why else do we live but to inspire and be inspired.

Cultural

It's important to be reminded of where we have been and where we could possibly go. Involving strong components of a countries culture is the bridge we should develop.

Key Ingredients

Researching the country and its cultural aspects. Everything from the businesses to the way their houses and clothing are done and worn. This will represent the ornaments of the building as many if not all of the traditional architecture of Turkey is decorated with ornamental designs. For the physical shape of the building I am combining aspects of the national plant which is a Tulip and the national flag which is a crescent moon and a star right of its center.




Named for its place of origin, Turkey, award-winning Tulip turkestanica is a wild-looking species with gray-green leaves and up to 12 star-shaped, white flowers borne on 10-inch stems (25 cm). ... Flowering in early spring, this tulip is a member of the Botanical Tulips and is quite versatile in the garden.




The flag of the Republic of Turkey, often referred to as the Turkish flag (Turkish: Türk bayrağı) is a red flag featuring a white star and crescent. The flag is often called al bayrak (the red flag), and is referred to as al sancak(the red banner) in the Turkish national anthem. The current design of the Turkish flag is directly derived from the late Ottoman flag, which had been adopted in the late 18th century and acquired its final form in 1844. The measures, geometric proportions, and exact tone of red of the flag of Turkey were legally standardised with the Turkish Flag Law on 29 May 1936.

The Solution

My solution is to use the elements of the Tulip and the Flag and present it in the form of a memorial or space of gathering of all walks of life to better udnerstand and learn the Turkish culture. After all they do make the best Kebabs.

Each Culture will be represented the same way. We acquire the main ingredients and incorporate that in the design of each significant structure.
